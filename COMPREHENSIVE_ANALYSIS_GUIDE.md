## 📋 EXAMPLE: THYROID REPORT

### Brother Uploads: `brother_thyroid_report.jpg`

**WHAT IS PRESENT (Findings):**
```
✓ Abnormal TSH levels (elevated or decreased)
✓ Abnormal T3/T4 hormone levels
✓ Thyroid antibodies indicating autoimmune thyroiditis
✓ Signs of thyroid dysfunction on physical exam
```

**WHAT TO DO NEXT (Action Plan):**
```
Step 1: Start thyroid hormone replacement therapy immediately
  → Use Levothyroxine if hypothyroid

Step 2: Get TSH levels checked after 6-8 weeks
  → Adjust dosage based on results

Step 3: Take blood test every 6 weeks
  → Until levels stabilize

Step 4: Once stabilized, monitor TSH annually
  → Maintain routine follow-ups

Step 5: Avoid iron/calcium supplements
  → Take 4 hours before/after medication

Step 6: Take medication on empty stomach
  → 30 mins before breakfast (very important!)
```

**DIET PLAN:**

**Foods to Eat:**
- Iodine-rich: Seaweed, fish (salmon, tuna), eggs, dairy
- Selenium-rich: Brazil nuts, mushrooms, sunflower seeds
- Zinc-rich: Pumpkin seeds, cashews, chickpeas
- Iron-rich: Spinach, lentils, beef (4 hours from meds)
- Whole grains: Brown rice, oats, millets
- Fruits: Berries, apples, oranges

**Foods to Avoid:**
- Goitrogenic (raw): Broccoli, cabbage, cauliflower (cook well!)
- Soy products: Tofu, soy milk (interferes with absorption)
- Excess cruciferous: Kale, Brussels sprouts
- High-fiber with medicine: Separate by 4 hours
- Processed foods and excess caffeine

**Meal Timing:**
```
Take medicine 30 mins before breakfast
Eat 1 hour after medicine
Example: 7:30am medicine, 8:30am breakfast (fish with rice)
```

**Daily Recommendation:**
```
Balanced meals with iodine, selenium, and zinc
Example: Grilled fish + rice + steamed vegetables
```

**MEDICATIONS (As Before):**
```
OTC:
- Calcium supplement (4 hours apart from thyroid meds)

Prescription:
- Levothyroxine 25-200mcg daily (empty stomach)
- PTU 50mg 3x daily (for hyperthyroidism)
- Methimazole 5-20mg daily (alternative)
```

## 💊 EXAMPLE: DIABETES REPORT

### Upload: `diabetes_glucose_test.jpg`

**WHAT IS PRESENT:**
```
✓ Fasting blood glucose >126 mg/dL
✓ Random blood glucose >200 mg/dL
✓ HbA1c >6.5% (3-month average glucose)
✓ Signs of hyperglycemia (excessive urination, thirst)
✓ Increased infection risk and delayed healing
```

**WHAT TO DO NEXT (First 3 Steps):**
```
Step 1: Start Metformin 500mg twice daily immediately

Step 2: Check blood glucose 4 times daily
  - Morning (fasting)
  - Before lunch
  - Before dinner
  - Before bed

Step 3: Get HbA1c test every 3 months
  - Track 3-month average glucose
```

**DIET PLAN:**

**Foods to Eat:**
```
- Low glycemic: Beans, lentils, chickpeas, nuts
- Vegetables: Broccoli, spinach, peppers, carrots (non-starchy)
- Whole grains: Brown rice, millets, oats (portion control)
- Lean proteins: Chicken, fish, eggs, tofu
- Healthy fats: Olive oil, nuts, seeds (moderation)
- Low-sugar fruits: Apples, berries, oranges (limited)
```

**Foods to Avoid:**
```
- Refined carbs: White rice, white bread, sugary cereals
- Sugary drinks: Soda, fruit juices, energy drinks
- Sweets: Cakes, cookies, ice cream, candy
- Processed foods: Snacks, fast food, fried foods
- High-sugar fruits: Mango, banana, grapes (excess)
- Alcohol: Increases blood sugar fluctuations
```

**Daily Recommendation:**
```
1200-1500 calorie diet
Balanced plate: 1/4 protein + 1/4 carbs + 1/2 vegetables

Example Breakfast:
- Vegetable omelette with 2 eggs
- 1 whole wheat toast
- 1 cup low-fat milk
```

## 🫀 EXAMPLE: BLOOD PRESSURE REPORT

### Upload: `blood_pressure_reading.jpg`

**WHAT IS PRESENT:**
```
✓ Systolic BP above 140 mmHg or Diastolic above 90 mmHg
✓ Increased risk of heart attack and stroke
✓ Left ventricular hypertrophy (enlarged heart)
✓ Albumin in urine (kidney damage risk)
✓ Elevated cholesterol levels
```

**WHAT TO DO NEXT (First 4 Steps):**
```
Step 1: Start Lisinopril 10mg daily immediately
  → ACE inhibitor to lower BP

Step 2: Check BP at home daily
  → Record morning and evening readings
  → Keep log for doctor

Step 3: Get baseline ECG and kidney function test
  → Check for complications

Step 4: Reduce salt intake immediately
  → Target: Less than 6g/day (1 teaspoon)
```

**DIET PLAN (DASH Diet):**

**Foods to Eat:**
```
- Low sodium vegetables: Spinach, broccoli, carrots, peppers
- Potassium fruits: Bananas, oranges (lower BP naturally)
- Whole grains: Oats, brown rice, whole wheat
- Lean proteins: Fish (salmon with omega-3), chicken, eggs
- Healthy fats: Olive oil, nuts, seeds (moderation)
- Low-fat dairy: Yogurt, low-fat milk (calcium)
```

**Foods to Avoid:**
```
- High salt: Processed meats, canned foods, pickles, soy sauce
- Fried foods: Fast food, deep-fried items
- High-fat dairy: Full-fat milk, cheese, butter, cream
- Sugary items: Sodas, sweets, pastries (increase weight)
- Alcohol: Raises BP significantly
- Excess caffeine: Max 1-2 cups daily
```

**Daily Recommendation:**
```
DASH Diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)
Eat smaller portions 5 times daily
Include potassium with each meal for BP control
Max salt: 6g/day

Example Meal:
- Grilled salmon (omega-3)
- Brown rice
- Steamed broccoli with olive oil
- Fresh orange (potassium)
```
